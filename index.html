Website Redesign Proposal

Context, scope, information architecture, process & investment

1. Context & intent

Remarcable’s current site already carries a strong visual identity: large photography, generous white space, and a gallery-like calm that fits a premium interior design studio. The goal of this project is not to erase that identity, but to refine and re‑orchestrate it so the site better reflects:

  • Your human-centric, psychology-driven design philosophy.
  • Your three core segments: Healthcare, Workspaces, and Homes.
  • Your desire to nurture relationships over time, not just capture “ready to buy” visitors.

The redesign will focus where it matters most, through an iterative process with you at each step. The outcome: a cleaner, more conversion-focused, easier-to-manage experience, ready for Webflow.

2. High-level scope

Website purpose

  • Serve as a calm, premium gallery of your work and philosophy.
  • Nurture leads: keep Remarcable top-of-mind through content (blog, guides, tools) even when visitors are not yet ready to start a project.
  • Support decision-making: give prospective clients enough clarity on what you do, how you work, and what results you create to feel confident booking a consultation.
  • Turn visitors into consultation bookings or newsletter subscribers.

Who you are (and how you work)

  • Small team of experts, with deep experience and long-term, personal relationships with clients.
  • Not "just interior decorators", but human-centric interior architects, grounded in psychology, light, circadian rhythm, and biophilic design, focused on measurable business outcomes (patient experience, productivity, growth, brand perception).
  • You value timeless, architectural, editorial aesthetics, white space, subtle structure (not boxy "section blocks"), and a collaborative, reference-driven design process.

Segments you serve

  • Healthcare (Dental & Healthcare).
  • Workspaces (Commercial offices, co-working).
  • Homes (Residential; initially a soft launch).

These are segments, not separate businesses. The new site will let each type of visitor see themselves quickly, without three parallel site structures.

3. Information architecture

A lean, segment-aware IA will form the foundation of the redesign.

Top-level navigation

  • Home
  • Work (portfolio, clients, videos)
  • Services (process, steps, options)
  • Academy (content hub: articles, guides, calculators)
  • About (who we are, how we work, values)

Contact remains a dedicated page, reached via CTAs (“Book your consultation”, “Talk to us about your space”) and the footer, not as a main nav item.

Segments as filters (not extra pages)

  • Healthcare (Dental & Healthcare)
  • Workspaces (Commercial)
  • Homes (Residential)

These appear as three choices in the Home hero and as filters on Work and Services (and optionally Academy). One Services page; segments re-focus content, not multiply pages.

4. Page roles and proposed changes

Home – gallery-like front door

  • Segment hero: Healthcare · Workspaces · Homes; choosing one sets a segment filter for the session.
  • Awards and trust integrated into hero/composition (overlay or woven into imagery).
  • Specialism simplified: less click-to-reveal, clear value per segment at a glance.
  • Testimonials preview remodelled for mobile-first (photos and quotes preserved).
  • 3-step process simplified or replaced by a clear "How we work" link into About.

Work – portfolio with meaningful filters

  • Single Work page with segment filters (Healthcare / Workspaces / Homes) and tags (reception, treatment room, breakout, biophilic, bespoke furniture, video, before/after).
  • Each project: large imagery, white space, short outcome-focused description, short testimonial quote with link to full testimonial.
  • Clients section reworked: 15× 5-star reviews, logos, and "happy clients" imagery in an editorial layout.

Services – one clear story

  • One Services page explaining First Steps → Design → Build & Aftercare once; segment filter drives examples, language, and calculator CTA.
  • "Click into three options per stage" removed or simplified to high-level overview with optional progressive disclosure.
  • 4 key elements and before/after content moved into About and Work/case studies.

Academy – elevated content hub

  • Academy hub: Articles (blog), Guides & Checklists, Tools (calculators) in one editorial-style experience, with filters by content type and optionally segment.
  • Design: large white-space layouts, strong typography, mixed media without clutter.

About – who you are, why you exist, how you work

  • One About page with sections: Our Why, How We Work, 4 Key Elements, Team & Story, plus optional micro-testimonials and awards.
  • Testimonials page (linked from About, Work, footer) for full stories and future video testimonials.

5. Design language & interaction principles

  • Keep: White space, gallery feel, calm architecture-forward mood; gradients and accents (e.g. purple/blue hover) where they support hierarchy.
  • Elevate: Scannable typography (shorter paragraphs, clearer headings); big white text over imagery with careful overlays; sparing micro-interactions (hover dots, tooltips, transitions).
  • Avoid: Boxy sectioning; overuse of accordions and click-to-reveal patterns.

6. Process & collaboration (4–6 weeks)

A 4–6 week, iterative process with clear phases and deliverables. Each phase concludes with your sign-off before the next begins. You can stop after any phase and keep all deliverables produced to that point.

Phase Duration Deliverables
1. Discovery & IA confirmation Week 1 Walkthrough of IA (Home, Work, Services, Academy, About + segments). Review of references (Benjamin Hardman, BoConcept, Richard Oxford, Sophie Paterson, etc.). Signed-off sitemap and key page list.
2. UX flows & wireframes Weeks 2–3 Low-fidelity wireframes for Home, Work (index + case study), Services, Academy (hub + article), About, Contact. Focus on storytelling order, segment filters, CTAs, trust elements. 1–2 review sessions. Approved wireframe set.
3. Visual design in Figma (desktop + mobile) Weeks 3–5 Design language applied to all key templates. Component library (headers, footers, cards, filters, testimonial blocks). Iterative feedback on layout, typography, interactions. Approved high-fidelity Figma file (desktop + mobile).
4. Refinement & Webflow handoff Week 5–6 Final polish on all screens. Handoff documentation for Webflow (styles, components, interaction notes). Optional: light support during implementation.

Throughout: You are part of the process; you are designers and the website is a reflection of your work and identity, so it's important for me as well to include you at every iteration, idea, share sections, links and inspiration and make this a collaboration.
