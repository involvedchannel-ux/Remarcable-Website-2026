Website Redesign Proposal

Context, scope, information architecture, process & investment

1. Context & intent

Remarcable’s current site already carries a strong visual identity: large photography, generous white space, and a gallery-like calm that fits a premium interior design studio. The goal of this project is not to erase that identity, but to refine and re‑orchestrate it so the site better reflects:

  • Your human-centric, psychology-driven design philosophy.
  • Your three core segments: Healthcare, Workspaces, and Homes.
  • Your desire to nurture relationships over time, not just capture “ready to buy” visitors.

The redesign will focus where it matters most, through an iterative process with you at each step. The outcome: a cleaner, more conversion-focused, easier-to-manage experience, ready for Webflow.

2. High-level scope

Website purpose

  • Serve as a calm, premium gallery of your work and philosophy.
  • Nurture leads: keep Remarcable top-of-mind through content (blog, guides, tools).
  • Support decision-making: give prospective clients enough clarity to feel confident booking a consultation.
  • Turn visitors into consultation bookings or newsletter subscribers.

Who you are (and how you work)

Small expert team (4 people), with deep experience and long-term, personal relationships with clients. Not “just interior decorators”, but human-centric interior architects, grounded in psychology, light, circadian rhythm, and biophilic design, focused on measurable business outcomes. You value timeless, architectural, editorial aesthetics, white space, subtle structure, and a collaborative, reference-driven design process.

Segments you serve

  • Healthcare — Dental & Healthcare clinics, beauty/medical clinics, hospitals, hair implant clinics.
  • Workspaces — Commercial offices, co-working, hybrid hubs (typically 2000+ sq.ft).
  • Homes — High-end, wellness-focused residential (initially a soft launch).

These are segments, not separate businesses. The new site will let each type of visitor see themselves quickly, without three parallel site structures.

3. Information architecture

A lean, segment-aware IA will form the foundation of the redesign.

Top-level navigation

  • Home
  • Work (portfolio, clients, videos)
  • Services (process, steps, options)
  • Academy (content hub: articles, guides, calculators)
  • About (who we are, how we work, values)

Contact remains a dedicated page, reached via CTAs and the footer, not as a main nav item.

Segments as filters (not extra pages)

Healthcare · Workspaces · Homes appear as three choices in the Home hero and as filters on Work and Services. One Services page; segments re-focus content, not multiply pages.

4. Page roles and proposed changes

Home

Segment hero (Healthcare · Workspaces · Homes). Awards and trust integrated into hero. Specialism simplified; testimonials remodelled mobile-first. 3-step process simplified or replaced by “How we work” link into About.

Work

Single Work page with segment filters and tags. Each project: large imagery, white space, short outcome-focused description, short testimonial quote with link to full. Clients section: 15× 5-star reviews, logos, editorial layout.

Services

One Services page: First Steps → Design → Build & Aftercare; segment filter drives examples and calculator CTA. “Click into three options per stage” removed or simplified. 4 key elements and before/after moved into About and Work.

Academy

Articles, Guides & Checklists, Tools (calculators) in one editorial-style hub. Filters by content type and optionally segment. Large white-space layouts, strong typography.

About

One About page: Our Why, How We Work, 4 Key Elements, Team & Story, micro-testimonials and awards. Testimonials page linked from About, Work, footer.

5. Design language & interaction

Keep: White space, gallery feel, calm architecture-forward mood; gradients and accents where they support hierarchy.

Elevate: Scannable typography; big white text over imagery with careful overlays; sparing micro-interactions.

Avoid: Boxy sectioning; overuse of accordions and click-to-reveal patterns.

6. Process & collaboration (4–6 weeks)

Each phase concludes with your sign-off before the next begins. You can stop after any phase and keep all deliverables produced to that point.

Phase Duration Deliverables
1. Discovery & IA confirmation Week 1 Walkthrough of IA. Review of references. Signed-off sitemap and key page list.
2. UX flows & wireframes Weeks 2–3 Low-fidelity wireframes for all key pages. 1–2 review sessions. Approved wireframe set.
3. Visual design in Figma Weeks 3–5 Design language applied to all templates. Component library. Approved high-fidelity Figma file (desktop + mobile).
4. Refinement & Webflow handoff Week 5–6 Final polish. Handoff documentation for Webflow. Optional light support during implementation.
